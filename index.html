THE THREE PILLARS

SpaceX operates three integrated, vertically-controlled businesses. Each reinforces the others: launch cadence powers constellation growth; global connectivity feeds real-time data to frontier AI; AI accelerates discovery that makes multiplanetary life possible.

FALCON & STARSHIP LAUNCH SYSTEMS

Falcon 9 is the most active orbital launch vehicle in history (~620 launches, >99% mission success). A single booster has been reflown up to 34 times. In 2025 alone: 165 Falcon 9 launches and 159 flight-proven booster recoveries. Falcon Heavy (11 launches, 100% success) is the most powerful operational rocket. Dragon spacecraft: first commercial cargo to ISS and first privately-built crew vehicle to the station — 78 astronauts from 20 countries flown since 2020 under NASA Commercial Crew Program. Starship (11 flight tests) brings full reusability at super-heavy scale with tower “chopstick” booster catch and 100+ t payload target. Starshield: dedicated secure LEO network for U.S. government and national security missions (Earth observation, encrypted comms, hosted payloads) under long-term contracts.

THE ROAD AHEAD — USE OF PROCEEDS
STARSHIP & LAUNCH INFRASTRUCTURE

Next-generation reusable super-heavy lift vehicle. Enables 20× increase in Starlink deployment capacity per launch and entirely new mission classes (lunar, Mars, massive LEO constellations).

AI COMPUTE & ORBITAL SYSTEMS

Expansion of terrestrial gigawatt clusters and development of space-based AI inference satellites. Sun-synchronous orbit + Starlink backhaul = virtually unlimited clean power for frontier models.

CONSTELLATION SCALING

Continued V3 / mobile satellite production and deployment to serve the 3+ billion people still unconnected and to support direct-to-cell partnerships with mobile network operators worldwide.

DUAL-CLASS CAPITAL STRUCTURE & CONTROL

Class A: 1 vote per share. Class B: 10 votes per share. Elon Musk (Founder, CEO, CTO & Chairman) will control the majority of voting power and the ability to elect a majority of the board through his Class B holdings. The Company will qualify as a “controlled company” under Nasdaq rules and intends to rely on exemptions from certain governance requirements. Net proceeds fund Starship infrastructure, AI compute (terrestrial + orbital), constellation scaling, and general corporate purposes. Share count and pricing remain subject to the final prospectus.

PRINCIPAL RISKS & CONSIDERATIONS
STARSHIP EXECUTION

Development, testing, and achieving the required launch cadence, reusability, and payload capacity for Starship remains a complex technical and regulatory challenge. Delays would impact growth plans across all three pillars.

CAPITAL INTENSITY

The business requires significant ongoing investment in launch infrastructure, satellite constellations, and especially AI compute. Future profitability depends on successful scaling and monetization of new capabilities.

REGULATORY & SPECTRUM

Launch licensing, spectrum allocation, orbital debris rules, and international regulatory approvals are critical and subject to change. Government contracts (Starshield, NASA) carry additional compliance requirements.

COMPETITIVE & TECHNOLOGY

Rapid advancement by competitors in launch services, satellite broadband, and frontier AI could affect market position. Continued leadership depends on maintaining extreme vertical integration and pace of innovation.

These are among the most material risks disclosed in the S-1. Investors should read the full “Risk Factors” section of the registration statement.
